Practice

Treatment

These guidelines can help you care for minor cuts and scrapes:

Step 1: Wash your hands. This helps avoid infection.

Step 2: Stop the bleeding. Minor cuts and scrapes usually stop bleeding on their own. If needed, gently press the wound with a clean bandage or cloth. Raise the area until the bleeding stops.

Step 3: Clean the wound. Rinse with water to reduce infection risk. Clean around the wound with soap but avoid getting soap inside. Use tweezers cleaned with alcohol to remove debris.

Step 4: Apply antibiotic or petroleum jelly to keep the surface moist and help prevent scarring. If you get a rash, stop using the ointment.

Step 5: Cover the wound with a bandage or gauze to keep it clean. Minor scrapes or scratches may not need covering.

Step 6: Change the covering at least once a day or if it becomes wet or dirty.

Seek medical care if:

- There are signs of infection like increasing pain, warmth, or swelling.

- A tetanus shot is needed (e.g., for deep or dirty wounds).

For further guidance, watch the video on how to clean a wound by Mayo Clinic.

Types of Cuts and Scrapes

Here are different types of cuts and scrapes you might encounter and how to treat them:

Minor Scrape

A small abrasion to the skin that may cause bleeding. Clean with water and apply an antiseptic ointment.

Deep Cut

A deeper wound that may require stitches. Apply pressure to stop the bleeding and seek medical help immediately.

Grazes

Shallow wounds typically caused by friction. Clean with water and apply a sterile bandage.

Incision

A clean cut made by a sharp object. Clean the wound and apply a sterile dressing.
